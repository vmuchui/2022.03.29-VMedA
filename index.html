about us

VMedA is an online (internet) based telehealth service that will help facilitate healthcare access through the platform which will act as a liaison between healthcare providers and the people in order to make sure their healthcare needs are met. Utilizing telehealth, patients will be able to see and communicate with specialists and sub-specialists in a timely manner while staying in the comfort of their home or local facility. Local healthcare providers can also benefit from sub-specialists' expertise provided via telehealth. Our Mission

VMedA’s mission is to improve the living standards of the people within Kenya through the provision of better access to medical services with the use of telehealth.

Our Vision

To become an international leader in provision of healthcare solutions to all people through access to quality, affordable treatment and aftercare. To facilitate, co-ordinate and make more accessible quality care, education and research that are patient-centered, reliable and timely.

Our Values

our services



VMedA provides the following services:

• Chronic care management interventions using telehealth to provide patients with access to integrated care during their primary care visits.

• Provider-to-provider models that allow rural primary care providers and specialist providers to work as a team in order to share knowledge and manage patient care. This may involve the use of live interactive video or the use of store and forward transmission of diagnostic images, vital signs and / or video clips along with patient data for later review.

• Access to emergency care providers in real-time for evaluations and consults to local providers.

• Home monitoring to engage patients in their homes between medical visits by helping them effectively manage their conditions.

• Medical education to provide continuing medical education credits for health professionals and special medical education seminars for targeted groups in remote locations.

• Telehealth technology will allow specialists located in urban areas to connect with residents in rural long-term care facilities with chronic health problems through remote patient monitoring, i.e., using devices to remotely collect and send data to a home health agency or a remote diagnostic testing facility (RDTF) for interpretation. Such application might include a specific vital sign, such as blood glucose or heart ECG or a variety of indicators for homebound patients. These services can be used to supplement the use of visiting nurses.

• Online therapy and remote counseling will link residents to urban behavioral health and mental health counseling services through video and phone in order for consumers to obtain specialized health information and online discussion groups to provide peer-to-peer support.

• Tele-pharmacy will extend access to pharmacy services, including medications and medication counseling, at local healthcare facilities and community pharmacies.

• Electronic communications will connect providers working in isolated areas to create virtual professional communities that can assist with patient care.

• Healthcare providers' use of mobile devices, such as tablets and smartphones, will improve communications with patients and other providers.

• Interpreter services can be transmitted on-demand through audio and/or visual technology for patients who speak limited or no English.

• School-based telehealth will increase access to pediatric care and, in some cases, increase access to telehealth for the broader community.
